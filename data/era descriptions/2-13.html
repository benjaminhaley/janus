02.13 Tumor and Lifetime Study on Rats After Acute Neutron Exposure and/or Treatment with Paradichlorobenzene and Tetrachlorobenzyltoluene

Institution: CEA, DSV-DPTE (IPSN) Fontenay-aux Roses, France

Scientists: M. Morin; G. Monchaux; 

Purpose: To assess the risks of neutrons in combination with a chemical carcinogen

Status: 1986 - 1990, data in ERAD

Treatment: Exposure to an about 100 msec flash of fission neutrons from the "SILENE" reactor (gamma/neutron ratio 1-40) and inhalation of para-dichlorobenzene (PCDB) or tetrachlorobenzyltoluene (TCBT); some groups received 6 injections of 25 mg/kg BNF at an interval of 15 days

Dosimetry: Double ionization chamber (Ar Te Gas) activation detectors (S, Ni, Mg, Cu, Au) passive semiconductor

Endpoints: Life-span study (spontaneous death) with macroscopic/microscopic pathology

Animal: Male and female Sprague-Dawley SPF rats aged 3 months
